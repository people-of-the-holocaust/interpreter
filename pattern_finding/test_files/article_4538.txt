ARTICLE: Project MUSE - OFFIZIERLAGER (OFLAG) IV A

The Wehrmacht established Oflag IV A (map 4e) in September 1939 then dissolved it on June 5, 1940. They reestablished it on August 31, 1940, and dissolved it again on November 20, 1941. The camp was located in the Hohnstein Castle in Hohnstein. Oflag IV A was subordinate to the Commander of Prisoners of War in Defense District IV (Kommandeur der Kriegsgefangenen im Wehrkreis IV).²
Oflag IV A’s first inmates were Polish officers and orderlies (682 and 98, respectively, as of December 13, 1939), but the Germans soon transferred them to Oflag VII A. Later the camp held French officers and orderlies (126 and 68 on September 10, 1940) and a few Belgians. The conditions were satisfactory and in keeping with the provisions of the Geneva Convention relative to the Treatment of Prisoners of War (1929).
